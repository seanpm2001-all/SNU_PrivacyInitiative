This page is derived from SourceBase and may contain some template elements. Make sure to point them out when found and use the latest version of the page.

Background on degoogling

Degoogling is a difficult process in 2020 due to Googles monopoly and ecosystem. I used to use Google heavily before late 2018. I used many different products, and after my turn for privacy, and new standards, I have gone about and worked on degoogling.

Even the first major versions of SNU were Google based unfortunately. I have successfully degoogled out of most products, the ones I personally find the most difficult to get rid of are Google Play, Android, and YouTube. I have abandoned my GMAIL, but unfortunately still have to use it sometimes.

The process of Degoogling is just taking Google out of your life. It is difficult, but hopefully someday it will be possible.

Alternates

Alternates to Google Drive

There aren't too many good alternates to Google Drive. If you are able to, you can set up your own file server.

If you want to jump ship and go to the Microsoft monopoly (like I had to do for now) you can try OneDrive.

OneDrive

Pros

* You have moved away from a Google product

* You have a working online file service

Cons

* You have entered/gone deeper into the Microsoft monopoly

* There is 10 gigabytes less free space available with free accounts on OneDrive than there is on Google Drive (OneDrive: 5GB, Google Drive: 15 GB)

* Your privacy is still at risk.

Alternates to Android

Android is currently the most popular operating system in the world. It has a massive 2,000,000,000+ (2 billion plus) user marketshare and is the dominant mobile operating system. Unfortunately, it is also a Google product.

The reason why a lot of people choose to stay with Android is because they don't want to get iOS and an iPhone/iPod/iPad. One of the reasons I switched back in 2014 was because my iPod 5 didn't have support for SD cards, and apple mobile devices at the time didn't have support for them (I think even today that is still the case, but I haven't checked recently)

Alternates to Google Chrome

Google Chrome is currently the most commonly used browser in the world, and it is also part of Googles monopoly. Despite lacking many W3 standard features, and having other issues, it is constantly being pushed out. Google Chrome is an easy one to get rid of (as long as you don't have ChromeOS)

Personally, I use Firefox. It does nearly everything good that Chrome does, and it isn't as memory intensive, and it is a lot faster. Mozilla Firefox has nearly a half billion users as of February 9th 2020

Mozilla Firefox

Firefox is a free and open source web browser.

Pros

* Everything good that Chrome does works with Firefox

* You don't have to deal with the Google Chrome monopoly

* There are no known privacy issues with Firefox

* Firefox is a very stable browser

* Firefox is not memory intensive

* Firefox is open source, opposed to being a freeware application

* Firefox is actively updated

* Firefox source code goes all the way back to Netscape

Cons

* None that I can currently find
